TGLZEulerAngles GLZVectorMath

The Euler angles are three angles to describe the orientation of a rigid body with respect to a fixed coordinate system.
They can also represent the orientation of a mobile frame of reference in physics or the orientation of a general basis in 3-dimensional linear algebra. Is used with TGLZQuaternion.

Remember :
- X = Roll = Bank = Tilt
- Y = Yaw = Heading = Azimuth
- Z = Pitch = Attitude = Elevation

TGLZHmgPlane GLZVectorMath

A plane equation. Defined by its equation A.x+B.y+C.z+D, a plane can be mapped to the homogeneous space coordinates, and this is what we are doing here.
The plane is normalized so in effect contains unit normal in ABC (XYZ) and distance from origin to plane.

WARNING : Create(Point, Normal) will allow non unit vectors but basically DON'T DO IT.
A non unit vector WILL calculate the wrong distance to the plane.

It is a fast way to create a plane when we have a point and a normal without yet another sqrt call.
